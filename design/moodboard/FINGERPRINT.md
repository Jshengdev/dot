# FINGERPRINT — the four pillars the lab builds toward (Johnny's words, distilled)

> Captured 2026-06-13 from Johnny while setting up the visual lab. This is the *intent* behind every
> asset we recreate — the "why it's his" that the frozen tokens can't encode. Read it before tuning a
> panel. It sits alongside [MOODBOARD.md](./MOODBOARD.md) (the references) and the
> [05-NEW-BRAND](../05-NEW-BRAND.md) law (the values).

## 1 · Innovative simplicity, with distinction
Ultra-simple, clear messaging — but never generic. Clean thin lines and strokes, a new medium brought
to life with the tech of AI. Keep it simple with complementary greys **or** full contrast. The goal is
simplicity that still reads as *distinctly mine* at a glance.

## 2 · Game-native delight, tastefully restrained
> "What is something I love that no one else does? UI and interactions that are very native to the
> feeling but are built upon games — translating that delightful experience you get playing a game into
> something tasteful, as a way of presenting information and knowledge."

The fingerprint is the ability to make **small interactions that are extremely delightful without
overdoing it** — never basic effects, always built on clean principles. Borrow the *feel* of game UI,
not its noise. (In the lab: the cursor moving the orb's key light; the count-up; the breathing dot.)

## 3 · Shapes that encapsulate what the information is asking for
> "I love the uniqueness of shapes as a way of not being too freaky but still encapsulating what the
> information is. Go deeper into the UX of each independent piece of information — what does it serve,
> how should it feel, how abstract should it be in its communication."

Each datum gets its own considered form. A number wants a stat; an object wants a card; a choice wants
chips. **"The content is like a world, and the world is something only I can build — recognizable
because it stands out from the crowd CONSISTENTLY."**

## 4 · Repetition trains the user
> "The beautiful part is how you're training the user to use your site — building it like it's a
> tutorial on how to interact and view the information."

Keep everything SIMPLE; abstract what it's about; let the action / diagram / visual / button speak for
itself because it builds on what's already familiar. **One unique primitive, applied consistently to
many things, becomes the recognizable fingerprint of the whole surface.** Design the primitive once;
repeat it until it teaches itself.

---

### The lab's job
Every panel in `packages/lab` is a place to get one primitive *right* against these four pillars, then
repeat it everywhere. Distinction comes from the primitive; recognizability comes from the repetition.

**Consistency anchors:** a clean sans (Onest) + a clean mono (IBM Plex / DM Mono) · one blue · one ink ·
light not lines · one small delight per surface, never overdone.
