# INTAKE-QUESTIONS.md — DOT intake / onboarding reference

DOT's job is **describe, not diagnose.** These questions help a person *tell it how they feel* (by voice or text) so DOT can shape it into a story a provider understands. Below: validated instruments (real item wording), the clinician story-shapes (OLDCARTS / SOCRATES / ROS), low-stigma open-ended prompts, and one short unified flow. **Bold = validated instrument. Plain = conversational adaptation.** DOT never scores or labels — it captures wording and lets the provider interpret.

---

## 1. Anxiety — **GAD-7** (validated)

Stem: *"Over the last 2 weeks, how often have you been bothered by the following problems?"* Response anchors: **Not at all (0) · Several days (1) · More than half the days (2) · Nearly every day (3).**

1. Feeling nervous, anxious, or on edge
2. Not being able to stop or control worrying
3. Worrying too much about different things
4. Trouble relaxing
5. Being so restless that it is hard to sit still
6. Becoming easily annoyed or irritable
7. Feeling afraid as if something awful might happen

Plus impairment item (shared with PHQ-9): *"If you checked off any problems, how difficult have these made it for you to do your work, take care of things at home, or get along with other people?"* (Not difficult at all / Somewhat / Very / Extremely.)
**GAD-2** = items 1–2 only (ultra-brief; use as the adaptive trigger). Sources: [Wikipedia GAD-7](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generalized_Anxiety_Disorder_7), [Dartmouth-Hitchcock GAD-7 PDF](https://www.dartmouth-hitchcock.org/sites/default/files/2021-02/gad-7-anxiety-scale.pdf), [Case Western PHQ/GAD instructions](https://case.edu/medicine/wellness-pathway/sites/default/files/2018-05/instructions.pdf).

## 2. Depression — **PHQ-9** (validated)

Same stem + same anchors (0–3, last 2 weeks):

1. Little interest or pleasure in doing things
2. Feeling down, depressed, or hopeless
3. Trouble falling or staying asleep, or sleeping too much
4. Feeling tired or having little energy
5. Poor appetite or overeating
6. Feeling bad about yourself — or that you are a failure or have let yourself or your family down
7. Trouble concentrating on things, such as reading the newspaper or watching television
8. Moving or speaking so slowly that other people could have noticed — or the opposite, being so fidgety or restless that you have been moving around a lot more than usual
9. Thoughts that you would be better off dead, or of hurting yourself in some way

**Item 9 = safety flag.** DOT must surface any non-zero response to a crisis/escalation path (988 in the US), never silently store it. **PHQ-2** = items 1–2 (adaptive trigger). Sources: [CHCS PHQ-9 PDF](https://www.chcs.org/media/phq_9_eng1.pdf), [Wikipedia PHQ-9](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PHQ-9), [PubMed PHQ-2/GAD-2](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/35258171/).

## 3. Pain / acute sickness — **PEG** (validated, ultra-brief) + **0–10 NRS**

**PEG**, 3 items, each 0–10, derived from the Brief Pain Inventory:

- **P** — *"What number best describes your pain on average in the past week?"* (0 = no pain, 10 = worst imaginable)
- **E** — *"What number best describes how, during the past week, pain has interfered with your enjoyment of life?"*
- **G** — *"What number best describes how, during the past week, pain has interfered with your general activity?"*

Sources: [Pain Management Collaboratory PEG](https://painmanagementcollaboratory.org/peg/), [Krebs et al. 2009, J Gen Intern Med](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11606-009-0981-1). For acute sickness, pair PEG with a **Review of Systems** sweep (below) to catch red flags.

## 4. Chronic illness — **PROMIS-29 v2.x** (validated)

Generic, condition-agnostic; 7 domains × 4 items, 5-point scale, mostly past-7-days: **depression, anxiety, physical function, pain interference, fatigue, sleep disturbance, social-role participation** (+ a single 0–10 pain-intensity item). Example item wording: anxiety — *"I felt fearful"*; depression — *"I felt worthless."* For chronic illness DOT tracks the same domains **over time** (the timeline) rather than a one-shot score. Sources: [NIH PROMIS](https://commonfund.nih.gov/promis), [PROMIS-29 overview](https://heartbeat-med.com/resources/promis-29/). Layer condition-specific PROs only when the condition is known.

## Story-shapes clinicians use (ask the story in this frame)

**OLDCARTS** — generic history of present illness:
**O**nset (*when did it start, sudden or gradual?*) · **L**ocation (*where is it?*) · **D**uration (*how long does it last?*) · **C**haracter (*what does it feel like — sharp, dull, burning?*) · **A**ggravating (*what makes it worse?*) · **R**elieving (*what makes it better?*) · **T**iming (*constant or comes and goes; time of day?*) · **S**everity (*0–10?*). Source: [Osmosis OLDCARTS](https://www.osmosis.org/answers/old-carts-history-taking-mnemonic).

**SOCRATES** — pain specialization:
**S**ite · **O**nset · **C**haracter · **R**adiation (*does it spread anywhere?*) · **A**ssociations (*any other symptoms with it?*) · **T**ime course (*how has it changed over time?*) · **E**xacerbating/relieving factors · **S**everity. Source: [Geeky Medics SOCRATES](https://geekymedics.com/the-socrates-acronym-in-history-taking/).

**Review of Systems** — quick red-flag sweep, esp. acute sickness. 14 systems; the high-yield one is **Constitutional**: fever, chills, fatigue, unintentional weight change, night sweats, appetite change. Source: [Maryland ROS](https://health.maryland.gov/pophealth/Documents/Local%20Health%20Department%20Billing%20Manual/PDF%20Manual/Section%20III/Review%20of%20Systems%20(ROS)%20.pdf).

## Open-ended, low-stigma prompts (conversational — say these out loud)

These open the door before any instrument; they elicit the *subjective* story in the person's own words. Validated agenda-setting research shows direct open prompts surface the real concern.

- *"So — what's been going on?"* (the opener; let them tell the first thing on their list)
- *"What made you decide to reach out now?"* (the trigger / why-today)
- *"What are you most worried it could be?"* (surfaces the fear, not just the symptom)
- *"How's this been affecting your day — sleep, work, the people around you?"* (function)
- *"Is there anything else on your mind?"* / *"What else?"* (catches the late-raised concern)
- *"Can you tell me a bit more about that?"* (the deepener — repeat as needed)

Tone rules for DOT: no clinical jargon, no labels, no "symptoms" framing first; normalize (*"a lot of people put this off — glad you're saying it"*); never react with alarm; one question at a time; mirror their words back. Sources: [CLOSLER 5 questions](https://closler.org/connecting-with-patients/5-questions-to-learn-more-about-your-patient), [NCBI Medical Interview](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK349/), ["What Else?" agenda-setting](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/7480306_What_Else_Setting_the_Agenda_for_the_Clinical_Interview).

## Unified conversational onboarding flow (short + human)

Voice-first. Target 4–6 turns, ~90 seconds. DOT *talks*, the person *tells*.

1. **Intro (voice, DOT speaks):** *"I'm here to help you put what you're feeling into words — your way. Nothing here is a diagnosis. Just talk. What's been going on?"*
2. **Free story (open):** capture the whole utterance verbatim. → branch on content.
3. **One framing follow-up (OLDCARTS/SOCRATES-shaped, pick 1–2 gaps):** e.g. *"When did this start?"* + *"What makes it better or worse?"* For pain add severity 0–10.
4. **Adaptive branch (≤2 instrument items, only the matched area):**
   - low mood / worry cues → **PHQ-2 + GAD-2** (items 1–2 each). If positive, gently extend toward full PHQ-9/GAD-7 later — and route PHQ item 9 to safety.
   - pain / acute → **PEG** (3 items) + Constitutional ROS check (fever/weight/night sweats).
   - long-standing condition → **PROMIS** domains relevant to what they raised, framed over time.
5. **Worry + impact (open):** *"What are you most worried about?"* + *"How's it affecting your day?"*
6. **Close + consent:** *"Anything else? … I'll turn this into a short story you can share with your provider — want to hear it back?"*

Branch only into the area the person actually raised; never run all four. Keep instruments invisible — phrase items as conversation, not a form.

---

## What to capture per turn (feeds `stories{subjective, objective, delta, timeline}`)

| Field | What goes in it | Source in flow |
|---|---|---|
| **subjective** | the person's own words — verbatim transcript of the story, worry, and feeling | turns 2, 5 (open prompts) |
| **objective** | extracted structured facts: OLDCARTS/SOCRATES slots (onset, location, character, severity 0–10, aggravating/relieving, timing, radiation, associations), instrument items + raw responses (GAD-2/PHQ-2/PEG/PROMIS, each tagged with instrument name + item #), ROS positives | turns 3–4 |
| **delta** | DOT's neutral framing of *felt* (subjective) vs *recorded* (objective / prior events) — e.g. *"you said it's mild; you've mentioned this 4 weeks running"* — never a verdict | computed post-turn |
| **timeline** | onset date, episode timestamps, "why now" trigger, per-domain trend across sessions (chronic-illness PROMIS deltas live here) | turns 2–3 + cross-session |

**Capture rules:** tag every stored item with its provenance — `instrument:GAD-7#1` vs `conversational` — so the provider knows what's a validated score vs the person's narrative. Never compute or display a clinical cutoff to the user. Any PHQ-9 item-9 / self-harm signal → escalation path, logged, surfaced, never buried.
