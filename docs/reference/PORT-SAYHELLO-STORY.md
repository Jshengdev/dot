## 1. The framework sayhello used

**The soul (THESIS):** *"The information is never the problem; the information is never the solution. The
story is. A story carries far more, far more densely, than a giant blurb of data."* Everything else is
scaffolding for that one claim. The product turns scatter into **a story, not a dump** — and that's also how
you pitch it. **The pitch is told in the same beats as the product**, so judges (and customers) repeat it back.

**The 5-beat customer-as-hero spine** (the lead is the protagonist, *never* the team):
1. **THE GOAL** — what the hero is trying to do, in their own words.
2. **THE OBSTACLE** — the pain, grounded in real signal (the spam-blocked launch, the 30%-wrong data).
3. **THE OLD WAY FAILS** — why their current approach is no longer good enough (grounded, never invented).
4. **THE BETTER PATH** — how *we* create it (proof points / receipts only).
5. **THE BETTER OUTCOME** — specific, forward-looking, **offers the hero agency** ("a powerful position").

**Turning data into story:** *"Every beat grounds in a Signal or it gets cut."* The judge even scores a
`narrative_arc` axis — 1.0 if all 5 beats are present and sourced; capped at 0.5 *"if the seller is the main
character or a beat is invented."* So the discipline is structural: protagonist = the user, every claim = a
receipt, or it's deleted.

**The pitch beats** (PITCH-3MIN, timed): `[0:00] problem, dead clear` → `[0:20] why it matters + the human
truth` → `[0:40] what it is + the key difference` → `[0:55–2:15] the demo, where THE CATCH is the moment` →
`[2:15] why we're different (the architecture IS the moat)` → `[2:45] close`. The single line repeated 3x:
*"Most GTM AI is one model that trusts itself. We're a harness that verifies itself."* And the **hero moment is
sacred** — DEMO-SCRIPT: *"the catch is the moment,"* the one beat *"the judge walks away remembering,"* the
only thing that gets full polish.

## 2. How each sponsor was woven into the story (the SPONSOR-STORY pattern)

The rule: **one line per sponsor — what it does in the product + the *innovative angle* — and you point at its
node as it lights on screen.** Each sponsor is a *character with a role*, not a logo:

- Firecrawl = **the eyes** · ClickHouse = **the truth source** · Composio = **the reach** · OpenRouter = **the
  two minds** · Langfuse = **the glass** · OpenUI = **the voice** · Render = **the stage**.

Each line names the *non-obvious* use (*"most teams use ClickHouse to STORE their own data; we use its public
data as PROOF"*). Then it all collapses into **one architecture sentence** you can say in a breath:
*"Firecrawl sees it, ClickHouse proves it, Composio reaches for more, two OpenRouter models argue until the
story is true, Langfuse shows the receipts, OpenUI tells it, and Render ships it — a harness that catches
itself lying before you ever hit send."* That sentence *is* the moat pitch: the stack isn't decoration, it's
the differentiation.

**Port for DOT:** give every CORE sponsor a one-word *role noun* tied to DOT's truth-telling job (e.g. the
voice that hears the story, the source that grounds it, the mind that reflects it, the glass that shows the
work, the stage that ships it). Write one line each: role + the innovative angle. Then write DOT's single
architecture sentence and repeat it. Point at each node as it lights (per `docs/SPONSORS.md`, named on screen).

## 3. How it maps to DOT

**DOT's story (same spine):** anxiety **distorts your own account of yourself** → you tell DOT your story
(voice/text) → DOT reflects the **objective truth** of it back → you regain agency *and* your provider finally
sees what's real. The founder beat is real and load-bearing: **Johnny's own anxiety** — DOT is the tool he
needed. Use it the way sayhello used "I'm an AI-automations marketer" — first-person, lived, specific.

**The user is the hero. DOT is the harness for the self.** sayhello caught the *agent* lying; DOT catches
**anxiety** lying — the distortion in your self-narrative — and won't let a distorted account stand
unchallenged against the truth.

**DOT's 3-min demo, in sayhello's structure:**
- **[0:00–0:20] THE PROBLEM, first-person.** Anxiety doesn't just make you feel bad — *it edits the story you
  tell about yourself, with total confidence* (mirror sayhello's "it gets things wrong with total confidence").
- **[0:20–0:40] WHY IT MATTERS + human truth.** You can't out-reason a distortion from inside it; and your
  provider only ever hears the distorted version. The fix isn't "calm down" — it's **a system that reflects
  what's objectively true.**
- **[0:40–0:55] WHAT IT IS + the difference.** DOT hears your story and reflects the objective truth back —
  not a chatbot that agrees with you, **a mirror that grounds you.**
- **[0:55–2:15] THE DEMO — the CATCH is the moment.** User tells a real (or cached) story. DOT plays it back as
  the distorted self-account... **then the hero beat: DOT surfaces the objective truth the anxiety erased** —
  on screen, live. That single beat (MISSION-INTAKE blank #4) gets all the polish.
- **[1:40] The human's yes → out.** Hold-to-confirm; the grounded account blooms; *"the human takes it from
  here"* → and the provider gets the real picture.
- **[2:15] WHY DIFFERENT.** A bigger model can't fix this — it'd just agree more fluently. DOT is different
  because **reflecting objective truth is an architecture** (the sponsor stack), not a sympathetic prompt.
- **[2:45] CLOSE.** A tagline in sayhello's "before you say hello, know their story" register — e.g.
  *"Before anxiety tells your story, DOT tells the truth of it."* (Tune in `docs/DEMO-SCRIPT.md`.)

**The discipline that makes it land:** protagonist = the user (cap the score if DOT becomes the hero); every
reflected truth = a receipt, or it's cut; the pitch is told in the exact beats the product runs; one
architecture sentence, repeated. Story > data dump — *because a story is denser than a blurb*, and a person's
true self-account is the densest story there is.
