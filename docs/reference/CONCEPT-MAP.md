**The positioning line:** *"DOT isn't another AI you talk to instead of a person. It's the AI that helps a person finally understand you."*

## Handle-with-care (the two-eyes flags)
- **"Feel normal" must normalize the ACT of reaching out — not minimize the FEELING.** "Everyone struggles" read as
  *erasure* to real sufferers (Empathize). Knife-edge.
- **"Objective truth" is precise:** it's the *de-catastrophized self-record + behavioral data*, not omniscient truth.
  Don't overclaim.
- **"AI told someone to die"** is real and powerful but volatile in a healthcare room — frame as the failure designed
  against, with the human-in-the-loop as the answer; never a competitor dunk.
- **"Belong not special" + disability framing:** some people embrace their identity (disability pride). DOT is about
  *being seen/heard*, never "erase what's different." Keep it to dignity, not restoration.
- **The delta must stay observations, never a verdict** — never "you're overreacting." That's the line the whole
  thing lives or dies on (and the proven run got it right).
