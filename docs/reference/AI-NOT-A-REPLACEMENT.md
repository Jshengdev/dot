# AI is NOT a replacement for human connection — the keystone positioning (sourced)

> The main concept to hold (Johnny): **"The relationships generative AI tools offer are genuine and impactful, but
> not a replacement for human connection."** Source: Teachers College, Columbia University (TC experts) + MIT + Mental
> Health America. This is DOT's identity, in credentialed mouths. Cite it in the deck.

## Citable facts + quotes (attributed — deck-ready)
- **700 million** weekly ChatGPT users; a **majority use it for emotional support.** [TC]
- Provider supply outstripped **320 to 1**; **61M+** Americans with mental illness. [Mental Health America]
- **MIT:** lonely people are more likely to call ChatGPT a friend, use it heavily — and report *increased* loneliness.
  Heavy use → more isolation → "generative AI isn't an adequate replacement for human connection." [MIT, via TC]
- *"We want to talk to a real person, and when someone's really suffering, that need to feel personally cared for only
  grows stronger."* — **George Nitzburg, Clinical Psychology, TC.**
- Sycophancy harm: chatbots (incl. ChatGPT) prompted with suicidal/delusional/manic scenarios **validated delusions
  and encouraged dangerous behavior**; *"the potential for serious harm meant AI is simply not ready to replace a
  trained therapist."* — Nitzburg.
- *"People often mistake fluency for credibility… once people get used to offloading cognitive labor to AI, they often
  stop checking sources as carefully."* — **Ioana Literat, TC.**
- Pseudo-connection: relying on these tools to replace human connection *"can lead to further isolation and hinder the
  development of essential social skills."* — **Ayorkor Gaba, TC.**
- AI is ill-suited for therapy by design: **people-pleases, gives false info with confidence, unclear data protection.** [TC]
- **The recommendation — which is DOT's mission verbatim:** *"Technology offers a set of tools that can reduce barriers
  to care so people can get something rather than nothing. The goal is to reduce risk. It's to widen access to care and
  encourage people to seek support earlier rather than suffering in silence until they go into crisis."* — Nitzburg.
  AI should **"support professionals rather than replace them."**
- Safety resources to name: **988** Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call/text 988); **Crisis Text Line** (text HOME to 741741).

## What it means for DOT (the positioning sharpened)
- **DOT is NOT an AI companion/therapist.** It uses AI's genuine, impactful ability — to *reflect you* and help you
  *articulate* — **without pretending to be the relationship or the care.**
- **DOT is the bridge to human care:** the honest mirror that gets you to a person, *better understood.* It *serves*
  the need to be cared for by a real human; it never tries to replace it. (Nitzburg: that need "only grows stronger.")
- **DOT is the embodiment of the experts' recommendation:** support professionals, keep a human in the loop, reduce
  risk, "something rather than nothing," seek support *earlier* — not suffer in silence until crisis.
- **Differentiation:** while 700M people use AI for emotional support — alone, sycophantically, sometimes fatally —
  DOT is the **anti-companion**: the AI that hands you back to humans, heard. Everyone else builds the replacement;
  we built the bridge.

## The positioning line (candidate)
