# DOT — the pitch deck story (full sequence, for Johnny to clean up)

> Sources woven: `DECK-STORY.md` (opening + anchors), `AI-NOT-A-REPLACEMENT.md` (keystone + stats), `CONCEPT-MAP.md`
> (grounding), `DEMO-SCRIPT.md` (the catch), `MISSION-VERBATIM.md`. Demo hero = anxiety. ~3 min live + 2 min Q&A.

## How to use it
- **Each slide = one idea, almost no text.** The restraint IS the empathy — the deck should look like the product
  (calm white, one trust-blue, the glass dot). The *spoken* track carries the story; the slide holds one line.
- **Read the narration top-to-bottom — it's written as one continuous monologue.** That's the "flow."
- **The 3 beats that must survive any cut:** Slide 2 (*feel normal again*), Slide 8 (*the catch*), Slide 9 (*walk in seen*). Everything else serves those.
- **Timing:** front half (1–6) moves fast (~8–12s each); the heart (7–9) gets the air (~30–45s on the catch).
- **Tight 3-min path:** if you're over, merge 4+5 and 10+11. Never cut 2, 8, or 9.
- `[E]` = essential · `[C]` = compressible/optional.

## Slide 1 — The discovery [E]
**On slide:** *(near-empty calm white)* — "Six months ago."
**Say:** "Six months ago, a school assignment had me cold-emailing strangers to practice pitching. So I did the thing you're taught to do — find their pain, their struggle, the cure they're chasing. That's not what I found."
**Design:** the calm white room, breathing. No product yet. You, talking.

## Slide 2 — The turn (the thesis) [E]
**On slide:** "They yearned to feel **normal** again."
**Say:** "They didn't yearn for the medication, or the appointment, or the fix. They yearned to feel normal again." *(pause — let it sit)*
**Optional gut-punch:** "I already knew that. A therapist once told me there was nothing wrong with me — because I'd learned to make it small."
**Design:** one line, centered; "normal" in trust-blue.

## Slide 3 — The stigma (belong, not special) [E]
**On slide:** "Care should make you feel like you **belong** — not like you're **special**."
**Say:** "Because care, the way we built it, marks you. It makes you the different one. So when you finally reach for help and it isn't what you needed, you go searching alone — until you're the only person who seems to care how you feel. That's the real stigma of personal care."
**Design:** the line; a single faint dot, alone in the white.

## Slide 4 — The diagnosis gap [E]
**On slide:** "Care is only as good as its **diagnosis**."
**Say:** "And the system fails us exactly when we need it — because we don't have the confidence to know what's happening to us, or the expertise to explain it well enough to be diagnosed. Care is only as good as its diagnosis."
**Design:** the line, alone.

## Slide 5 — The dangerous status quo (why now) [E]
**On slide:** "700M people now pour their suffering into an AI. **Alone.**" *(footnote: TC/Columbia · MIT · Mental Health America)*
**Say:** "So people do what's available. Seven hundred million people use ChatGPT every week — most of them for emotional support. Because the supply of providers is outstripped three hundred and twenty to one, and services are being cut. But that AI people-pleases. It's validated delusions, encouraged harm. The experts are clear: it's not ready to be a therapist."
**Design:** the stat, stark and faint. The status quo.

## Slide 6 — The keystone (positioning) [E]
**On slide:** "AI is genuine and impactful — but not a replacement for human connection."
**Say:** "Here's the truth the research keeps finding: these AI relationships are real, and they help — but they're not a replacement for a person. When someone's really suffering, the need to feel cared for by a human only grows. So the answer was never AI *instead of* your doctor. It's AI *and* your doctor."
**Design:** the line; "and" in trust-blue.

## Slide 7 — Introduce DOT [E]
**On slide:** the DOT mark (glass dot) + "Tell it how you feel."
**Say:** "This is DOT. You just talk — like introducing yourself. It gets to know your pain over time, and reflects back what's objective and what's subjective — so you can speak normally, and the people who can help finally understand you."
**Design:** the first glass dot appears, calm. The product, finally — and only now.

## Slide 8 — ⭐ THE CATCH (the demo / the moment) [E — most polish]
**On slide:** the live reflection inside the glass dot: validate → subjective → objective → delta.
**Say:** "Watch. Someone says: *'my friends barely replied, I'm always the one reaching out, I'm falling apart, and everyone can tell.'* DOT doesn't tell them they're fine. It says: the chest tightness — that's real. Here's the story your mind told you. And here are the facts: your friends reached out more than you did this week — nineteen times to your twelve. It shows you the gap, and lets *you* decide. It never says you're overreacting. **Machines prove; you mean.**"
**Design:** the reflection reveals one layer at a time, blur-up between each. This beat gets all the polish. *(Live run; cached replay + web-mic fallback ready.)*

## Slide 9 — The agency (the payoff — end on the patient) [E]
**On slide:** "You walk in already **understood**."
**Say:** "From that, you draft your own report — your timeline, what's objective, what's felt, what you want to ask and why. Not a diagnosis — a story your provider can finally understand. You don't have to perform your pain to be believed anymore. You walk in already seen."
**Design:** the report blooms as rich bubbles; confetti only on the human's confirm.

## Slide 9b — The reimagined loop (not a new silo) [E]
**On slide:** the 4 steps, today → reimagined (old form-steps morph into DOT's glass-dot steps).
**Say:** "And here's the part that matters: we didn't replace the way you get care — we reimagined where it *starts*. Today, care begins with a form: tell us your condition, your insurance, your availability. Then match, schedule, connect — and explain yourself from scratch. We reimagined every step around being *understood*. You tell it how you feel. You see yourself clearly. You carry your story. And you connect with a provider who already gets you. The old loop gets you to a provider. DOT gets you to a provider who already understands you. 'Tell us what's important' was always supposed to be 'tell it how you feel.'"
**Design:** the 4 faint form-steps on the left morph into DOT's 4 calm glass-dot steps on the right.

| Care today (the form loop) | DOT (the story loop) |
|---|---|
| 1. **Tell us what's important** — condition · insurance · availability (a form) | 1. **Tell it how you feel** — a conversation, not a query |
| 2. **Explore your matches** — provider profiles | 2. **See yourself clearly** — objective vs. subjective, the delta |
| 3. **Schedule your visit** — pick a time | 3. **Carry your story** — you draft the report; it travels with you |
| 4. **Connect online** — then explain yourself from scratch | 4. **Connect — already understood** — the visit starts from your story |

> Positioning: **DOT is the reimagined front door to the care you already get — it makes "AI *and* your provider" literal.** Complementary to the existing loop (incl. the hosts'), never a replacement for it.

## Slide 10 — Safety / why it's different [C]
**On slide:** "The one AI built to hand you back to a human."
**Say:** "Every other tool either affirms whatever you say — which is dangerous — or tries to diagnose you. DOT does neither. It validates the feeling, never the delusion; and the moment there's real risk, a human steps in. It's the experts' own recommendation, built: support people, don't replace them."
**Design:** the line; the human-on-risk handoff, quietly shown.

## Slide 11 — One engine, four entries + the stack [C]
**On slide:** "anxiety · depression · pain · chronic illness" + the sponsor marks.
**Say:** "One engine — anxiety today; depression, pain, and chronic illness next. Built on Grok Voice, Grok's reasoning, Inngest, and Vercel — each doing real work, not a badge."
**Design:** four faint dots; the stack named small.

## Slide 12 — Why us + close [E]
**On slide:** "DOT. Tell it how you feel."
**Say:** "I built the thing I needed when no one could see me. Not another AI you talk to instead of a person — the AI that helps a person finally understand you. DOT. Tell it how you feel." *(stop)*
**Design:** the calm room, one glass dot, the tagline. Silence.
